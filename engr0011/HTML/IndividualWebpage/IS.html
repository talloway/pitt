Interesting Professional Society

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) is an professional society that is affiliated with both electrical and computer engineering, the latter of which I will pursue in the Swanson school.

  • Who are the members of this society?
    • Size of membership: 420.000
    • Members can be students or professionals, mostly in electrical and electronics engineering, but the organization has attracted professionals in many other engineering disciplines.
  • Why does this association exist?
    • Services: The IEEE provides learning opportunities within engineering and technology, such as the IEEE e Learning Library, a collection of online courses for self-paced learning.
    • Conferences: The IEEE sponsors more than 1,600 conferences and meetings worldwide each year.
    • Publications: IEEE produces over 30% of electrical and computer engineering literature, publishing over 100 peer-reviewed journals.
  • Becoming a member
    • I would join the IEEE because of its network of members around the world, which would give me opportunities to connect with a large number of professionals who are all doing research in the fields of electrical and computer engineering. Membership would be helpful to me as an engineer because I would have access to IEEE's many resources for conducting research in the field.
